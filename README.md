What is Vortex Media Fetcher?
Vortex Media Fetcher is a powerful, dark-themed Windows application designed to effortlessly pull media from across the web directly to your hard drive. Under the hood, it uses an advanced detection engine that automatically adapts to the website you are downloading from. Whether it needs to bypass TikTok's bot-protections by impersonating a mobile browser, or solve YouTube's background puzzles to extract high-definition encrypted audio, Vortex handles all the heavy lifting in the background.

Core Features
Universal Support: Downloads media from YouTube, TikTok, Instagram Reels, Twitter/X, Reddit, and hundreds of other platforms.

Format & Quality Control: Extract crisp 192kbps MP3 audio, or grab MP4 videos ranging from data-saving 480p up to the absolute Best Available quality.

Smart Codec Forcing: Automatically converts videos into the universal H.264 standard, ensuring your files play perfectly with full audio on default Windows media players.

Anti-Bot Bypasses: Utilizes Chrome browser spoofing and a lightweight JavaScript solver (Deno) to slip past aggressive website download blockers.

Modern, Fluid UI: Features a sleek dark mode, one-click clipboard pasting, live progress tracking, and custom save-folder selection.

How to Use Vortex Media Fetcher
Find Your Media: Copy the full URL of the video or audio track from your browser or app.

Paste the Link: Open Vortex Media Fetcher and click the Paste button next to the input bar.

Choose Your Settings: Use the dropdown menus to select your format (MP4 Video or MP3 Audio) and your desired video quality.

Set Your Folder: Click Choose Save Folder to pick exactly where you want the final file to land on your computer.

Fetch: Hit the Fetch Media button. The progress bar will track the download in real-time.

Play: Once the status turns green and says "Download Complete!", your file is fully processed, merged, and ready to play.

System Requirements
To unlock the full power of Vortex Media Fetcher, your system must have the following background engines installed:

FFmpeg: Required for merging separate high-definition video and audio streams together.

Deno: A JavaScript runtime required to solve background puzzles on sites like YouTube.
